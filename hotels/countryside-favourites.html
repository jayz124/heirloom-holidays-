Countryside Favourites

Explore the beautiful English countryside

Rolling hills, ancient woodlands, and centuries of history converge in England's most treasured country house hotels. These grand estates offer the perfect escape from city life—crackling fires, afternoon tea, world-class spas, and restorative walks through manicured gardens. A quintessentially British retreat awaits.

Grand estates and country house luxury

Surrey, England

Beaverbrook Hotel & Spa

The former home of press magnate Lord Beaverbrook, this Surrey Hills estate offers one of Britain's most glorious views from its terrace. Japanese cuisine in the garden, a hyperbaric oxygen therapy chamber, and impossibly cosy interiors make this grand mansion feel surprisingly modern.

Hampshire, England

Four Seasons Hampshire

A Georgian manor set within 500 acres of Hampshire countryside, complete with Sharkie's Reef family pool and resident Shetland ponies Milo and Dougal. Horseback rides through ancient woodlands and world-class spa treatments offer the perfect country escape.

London, England

Raffles London at The OWO

The Old War Office—Churchill's wartime headquarters—reborn as one of London's most significant hotel openings. Nine restaurants, a Guerlain spa, and rooms that blend historic grandeur with contemporary luxury. The ultimate base for exploring the English capital and beyond.

Historic Building 9 Restaurants Guerlain Spa

Berkshire, England

Cliveden House

This Italianate mansion has hosted royalty, aristocracy, and the Profumo affair. The 376-acre National Trust estate features the iconic Spring Cottage and heated outdoor pool, while afternoon tea on the terrace overlooks the Thames Valley's most celebrated views.

National Trust Thames Valley Historic Icon

London, England

The Connaught

A Mayfair institution where English heritage meets contemporary sophistication. Hélène Darroze's two-Michelin-starred restaurant, the legendary Connaught Bar, and impeccable service have made this the London address for discerning travellers since 1917.
