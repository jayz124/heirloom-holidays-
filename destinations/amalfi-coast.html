Italy

La Dolce Vita on the Amalfi Coast

Where dramatic cliffs meet the azure Mediterranean and every village tells a story of centuries past

Home / Destinations / Amalfi Coast
Back to Destinations

The Amalfi Coast is Italy at its most theatrical—a UNESCO World Heritage coastline where pastel-painted villages cling impossibly to vertiginous cliffs, lemon groves perfume the air, and the Mediterranean sparkles in shades of sapphire and turquoise. This is where la dolce vita was born, and where it continues to flourish in the most elegant way imaginable.

A Coastline of Dreams

From the glamour of Positano to the historic grandeur of Ravello, each village along this 50-kilometre stretch offers its own distinct character. Wander through narrow cobblestone lanes, discover hidden trattorias serving the freshest seafood, and watch the sunset paint the cliffs in shades of gold and rose from your private terrace.

The Amalfi Coast rewards those who take their time. Linger over a long lunch of local anchovies and handmade pasta. Charter a private boat to explore hidden coves and grottoes. Ascend to Ravello's legendary gardens for a concert at Villa Rufolo, where Wagner found his inspiration.

Capri & Ischia

No journey to the Amalfi Coast is complete without exploring the legendary islands that dot the Bay of Naples. Capri, with its glamorous piazzetta and the ethereal Blue Grotto, has captivated visitors from Roman emperors to modern-day celebrities. Meanwhile, Ischia offers a more tranquil escape, where natural thermal springs and lush gardens create an atmosphere of pure wellness.

The Art of Italian Living

The Amalfi Coast has been a muse to artists, writers, and composers for centuries. Visit the ceramic workshops of Vietri sul Mare, where artisans continue traditions dating back to the Renaissance. Explore the ancient paper mills of Amalfi itself, or simply lose yourself in the timeless rhythm of Italian coastal life—espresso at dawn, aperitivo at sunset, and starlit dinners that stretch into the small hours.

Where to Stay

Our handpicked selection of the coast's most exceptional properties, each offering a front-row seat to Mediterranean magic

Belmond Hotel Caruso, Ravello

Perched on the highest point of Ravello, this 11th-century palazzo offers what many consider the most spectacular views on the entire coast. The infinity pool, seemingly floating above the coastline, has become one of Italy's most iconic images. Gardens, frescoed ceilings, and Michelin-starred dining complete the experience.

Infinity Pool Historic Palace Michelin Dining

Le Sirenuse, Positano

The Sersale family has welcomed guests to this whitewashed gem since 1951, and it remains the quintessential Positano address. Every room offers views of the village or sea, while the Michelin-starred La Sponda restaurant, lit by 400 candles each evening, provides one of Italy's most romantic dining experiences.

Family-Owned Sea Views Spa & Wellness

Il San Pietro di Positano

Carved into the cliffs just outside Positano, Il San Pietro is a masterpiece of design and engineering. A private elevator descends to a secluded beach club, while terraces cascade down the hillside through bougainvillea-draped gardens. The restaurant terrace offers uninterrupted views of the coastline.

Private Beach Tennis Court Cliff-Side Setting

Palazzo Avino, Ravello

This 12th-century private villa turned luxury hotel combines aristocratic elegance with contemporary comforts. The pink-hued facade is a Ravello landmark, while inside, hand-painted tiles and antique furnishings create an atmosphere of refined Italian heritage. The Clubhouse by the Sea offers exclusive beach access.

Heritage Property Beach Club Rooftop Restaurant
