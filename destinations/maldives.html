Indian Ocean

Paradise Found in the Maldives

Where turquoise lagoons meet endless skies and time moves to the rhythm of the tides

Back to Destinations

The Maldives exists in a realm of impossible beauty—a constellation of 1,200 islands scattered across the Indian Ocean like pearls on silk. Here, the water shifts through every shade of blue imaginable, house reefs teem with marine life, and the concept of barefoot luxury reaches its zenith. This is the ultimate escape, a place where the rest of the world simply fades away.

Island Seclusion

Each resort in the Maldives occupies its own private island, creating an unparalleled sense of exclusivity and intimacy. Wake to the sound of waves lapping beneath your overwater villa, take a morning swim in your private infinity pool, and watch the sunset paint the sky in shades of amber and rose from your deck. The rhythm of island life is gentle and unhurried—exactly as it should be.

The Maldives pioneered the overwater villa concept, and nowhere does it better. These architectural marvels extend over crystalline lagoons, with glass floors revealing the marine life below and direct access to some of the world's most pristine waters. It's a way of living that feels like a dream made tangible.

Underwater Wonders

Beneath the surface, the Maldives reveals another world entirely. The house reefs that surround most islands offer some of the planet's finest snorkeling—swim with manta rays, spot reef sharks patrolling the channels, and drift alongside sea turtles in their natural habitat. For divers, the Maldives is a bucket-list destination, with vibrant coral gardens, dramatic channel dives, and the chance to encounter whale sharks in season.

Wellness & Renewal

The Maldives has become a global destination for wellness, with world-class spas offering treatments that draw on both Eastern and Western traditions. Over-water treatment pavilions, yoga at sunrise on private sandbanks, and holistic wellness programs designed by leading practitioners—this is rejuvenation taken to an entirely new level. Many resorts now offer dedicated wellness retreats, combining physical renewal with digital detox and mindful practices.

Where to Stay

The world's most exclusive island resorts, each offering its own interpretation of Maldivian paradise

Soneva Fushi

The original barefoot luxury resort, Soneva Fushi pioneered sustainable luxury in the Maldives. Hidden among dense tropical vegetation, the villas are designed to blend seamlessly with nature while offering every imaginable comfort. The resort's observatory, outdoor cinema, and exceptional dining—including the famous Japanese restaurant Shisa—make this a destination unto itself.

Sustainable Luxury Observatory 9 Restaurants

One&Only Reethi Rah

One of the largest islands in the Maldives, Reethi Rah offers space and privacy on a grand scale. The villas are among the most spacious in the country, with some beach villas featuring private pools and direct lagoon access. The resort's 12 stunning beaches mean you can always find your own private stretch of paradise.

12 Beaches Watersports Tennis & Fitness

Cheval Blanc Randheli

LVMH's first foray into hospitality brings Parisian elegance to the Indian Ocean. Designed by Jean-Michel Gathy, the resort occupies a pristine island in Noonu Atoll, offering the ultimate in contemporary luxury. The Art de Recevoir philosophy ensures every detail is perfect, from the exceptional dining to the innovative spa treatments.

LVMH Property Private Island Guerlain Spa

Four Seasons Landaa Giraavaru

Set within a UNESCO Biosphere Reserve, Landaa Giraavaru offers unparalleled access to the Maldives' marine wonders. The resort operates a Marine Discovery Centre and manta ray research programme, allowing guests to contribute to conservation efforts. The AyurMa spa, inspired by Ayurvedic and yogic practices, offers transformative wellness journeys.

Marine Centre Manta Rays AyurMa Spa
