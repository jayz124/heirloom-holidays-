Fire & Ice in Iceland

Where glaciers meet volcanoes and the Northern Lights dance across endless skies

Back to Destinations

Iceland defies expectation at every turn. This is a land where the earth still breathes—where geysers erupt on schedule, glaciers calve into lagoons, and volcanic landscapes stretch to infinity under skies painted with the aurora borealis. It's a place that humbles and inspires in equal measure, offering adventures that feel genuinely otherworldly.

The Golden Circle & Beyond

While the famous Golden Circle offers an accessible introduction to Iceland's geological wonders—the thundering Gullfoss waterfall, the erupting Strokkur geyser, and the tectonic drama of Þingvellir—the true magic lies in venturing further. The black sand beaches of Vík, the ethereal blue ice caves of Vatnajökull, and the remote Westfjords offer experiences that feel untouched by time.

In summer, the midnight sun creates endless days for exploring. In winter, the darkness brings its own gifts: the chance to witness the Northern Lights in their full glory, followed by a soak in a geothermal hot spring under a canopy of stars.

Geothermal Wonders

Iceland sits atop one of the most geologically active places on Earth, and this energy infuses every aspect of Icelandic life. From the famous Blue Lagoon to hidden hot springs known only to locals, the tradition of bathing in naturally heated waters is central to the culture. These geothermal pools offer more than relaxation—they're a connection to the primal forces that shaped this island.

Wildlife & Wilderness

Iceland's waters teem with life. Whale watching from Húsavík offers encounters with humpbacks, minkes, and occasionally blue whales—the largest creatures ever to exist. Puffin colonies dot the coastal cliffs in summer, while Icelandic horses, a breed unchanged since Viking times, roam the highlands. For the adventurous, helicopter tours reveal landscapes impossible to reach by road—volcanic craters, hidden valleys, and ice fields that stretch to the horizon.

Where to Stay

Exceptional lodges and hotels that bring you close to Iceland's raw beauty while providing refined comfort

The Retreat at Blue Lagoon

Built into an 800-year-old lava flow, The Retreat offers the most exclusive Blue Lagoon experience. Each suite features floor-to-ceiling windows overlooking the milky-blue waters and moss-covered lava fields. Private lagoon access, a subterranean spa carved from volcanic rock, and Moss Restaurant's tasting menus make this Iceland's most coveted address.

Private Lagoon Lava Spa Fine Dining

Ion Adventure Hotel

Perched on a lava field overlooking Þingvallavatn lake, Ion Adventure Hotel embodies sustainable luxury in one of Iceland's most dramatic settings. Floor-to-ceiling windows frame views of the Northern Lights in winter, while the outdoor geothermal pool offers year-round stargazing. The ideal base for Golden Circle exploration.

Northern Lights Geothermal Pool Sustainable Design

Deplar Farm, Troll Peninsula

In the remote Fljót Valley, this converted sheep farm offers Iceland's most exclusive adventure experience. Accessed by helicopter or super-jeep, Deplar provides heli-skiing, horse riding, and fly-fishing in summer, plus exceptional Northern Lights viewing in winter. The geothermally heated pool offers views of the surrounding mountains.

Heli-Skiing Remote Location All-Inclusive

Hotel Rangá

Set in the countryside of South Iceland, Hotel Rangá is purpose-built for Northern Lights viewing, with wake-up calls when the aurora appears. The log-cabin-style hotel sits on the banks of a salmon river, offering world-class fly-fishing alongside stargazing from their observatory. Continental suites themed after different regions add unique character.

Observatory Aurora Alerts Fly Fishing
