Tanzania

The Great Migration

Witness two million wildebeest thunder across the Serengeti in nature's greatest spectacle

Back to Destinations
By Kara, Heirloom Holidays Last updated March 2026

Tanzania is Africa's visual masterpiece—a country of natural splendour, astounding wildlife, and geological wonders. Africa's highest mountain, Kilimanjaro, rises from lush grassy plains and towers over the land, its snow-capped peak a sight to behold. But it's the Serengeti that captures the imagination of every true safari lover.

The Serengeti National Park, meaning "endless plains" in the local Maasai language, is undoubtedly one of the world's most celebrated wilderness areas. It's an ongoing source of inspiration to writers, filmmakers and photographers—and soon, to you and your family.

The Ultimate Safari Experience

The annual movement of wildebeest and other herbivores across the Serengeti-Mara ecosystem is rarely the same in precise timing and direction, but what remains constant is its breathtaking scale. Two million wildebeest, zebra and gazelle race for their lives, dodging crocodiles, hyenas and big cats with big teeth.

This is the showstopping event, but by no means the only exciting wildlife spectacle. Year-round, lions, cheetahs and spotted hyenas stalk Cape buffalo, waterbuck and giraffe wandering freely across the savannah. The Serengeti delivers extraordinary wildlife encounters in every season.

Frequently Asked Questions

When is the best time to go on safari in Tanzania?

The dry season from June to October offers the best wildlife viewing, as animals gather around water sources and vegetation thins. The Great Migration reaches the northern Serengeti around July–September. January and February are excellent for calving season in the southern Serengeti, with fewer crowds.

How much does a luxury Tanzania safari cost?

A luxury Tanzania safari typically costs between £5,000 and £15,000 per person for 7–10 nights, depending on the lodges, season, and experiences included. Peak season (July–October) commands higher rates. Heirloom Holidays often secures better pricing and added amenities through its trade partnerships.

Is Tanzania safe for a family safari holiday?

Tanzania is one of the safest safari destinations in Africa. Luxury lodges and camps maintain excellent safety standards, and experienced guides accompany all game drives. Most camps welcome children aged 6 and above, with some offering dedicated family programmes and child-friendly safari activities.

What is the Great Migration and when does it happen?

The Great Migration is the annual movement of over 1.5 million wildebeest, along with hundreds of thousands of zebra and gazelle, across the Serengeti-Mara ecosystem. It runs year-round in a continuous cycle: calving in the southern Serengeti (January–March), moving north through the western corridor (April–June), and dramatic river crossings in the north (July–October).
